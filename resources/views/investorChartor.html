Investor Charter

Page 1 of 5 | Last Updated: 01 January 2025

This Investor Charter has been prepared in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) to enhance transparency, protect investor interests, and promote ethical practices in the securities market.

The purpose of this charter is to inform investors about the services provided by intermediaries, their rights and responsibilities, and the grievance redressal mechanisms available to them.

Investors are advised to carefully read and understand this document before engaging in trading or investment activities through any registered intermediary.

This charter applies to all retail and institutional investors who participate in the securities market through stock brokers, trading members, or authorized intermediaries.

Vision & Mission

Page 2 of 5

Vision

To create a transparent, fair, and secure investment environment where investor interests are protected and trust is maintained at all times.

Mission

  • To provide efficient and reliable services to investors.
  • To ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.
  • To promote ethical conduct and professional integrity.
  • To resolve investor grievances in a timely and transparent manner.

The intermediary strives to continuously improve service quality through technology, investor education, and adherence to best practices.

Services & Rights of Investors

Services Provided

  • Execution of trades on recognized stock exchanges.
  • Settlement of trades as per prescribed timelines.
  • Issuance of contract notes and trade confirmations.
  • Maintenance of client accounts and records.
  • Providing access to grievance redressal mechanisms.

Rights of Investors

  • Right to receive accurate and timely information.
  • Right to fair and transparent dealings.
  • Right to privacy and confidentiality.
  • Right to prompt grievance redressal.
  • Right to protection against unfair trade practices.

Responsibilities, Do’s & Don’ts

Page 4 of 5

Responsibilities of Investors

  • Read and understand all documents before investing.
  • Ensure adequate knowledge of risks involved.
  • Keep KYC details updated.
  • Monitor transactions regularly.

Do’s

  • Deal only with registered intermediaries.
  • Verify trade details from official sources.
  • Maintain records of all transactions.

Don’ts

  • Do not share login credentials.
  • Do not sign blank documents.
  • Do not rely on unauthorized tips.
